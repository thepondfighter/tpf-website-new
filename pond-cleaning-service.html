Pond Cleaning Service

Originally starting out as a pond cleaning company, we have spent a lot of time figuring out what works and what doesn't when it comes to cleaning a pond. We've learned over the years that, ponds, depending on their environment, have different needs. For example, ponds that receive lots of sunlight require more surface-blocking plants than those that get lots of shade. Ponds built along slopes tend to accumulate more dirt than those built on flat ground. Before cleaning a pond, we consider variables like these and devise a proper strategy that caters to the pond's individual circumstance.

The importance of beneficial bacteria and microorganisms is universal to all ponds. Found within the ecosystem of the pond, these guys are what keep the algae down and the water clear. With this in mind, our goal is not only to clean the pond but also to do it in a way which conserves its microscopic inhabitants.

The general pond cleaning steps are listed below.

1. Drain the water

During this process, we save clean pond water for later use when refilling. Pond water is rich in good bacteria and microorganisms that help maintain the health of the pond. Additionally, saving this water allows us to return the fish to the water that they are used to, minimizing their stress.

2. Relocate the fish

When the pond is drained down to about 6 to 8 inches, we can start to relocate the fish to an exterior holding tank. Having decades of fish keeping experience, we take pride in our ability to safely rehome fish for the duration of the job.

3. Power wash top to bottom

We use a low power pressue washer to extract algae and debris from the rocks of the pond. Not only does this preserve the beneficial bacteria found among these rocks, but it also prevents liner damage in bare-bottom ponds.

4. Trim overgrown vegetation

Although plants are an essential component of any healthy pond, many of them have a tendency to become excessive. Water lilies and irises, for example, are frequently pruned in order to bring balance to the pond.

6. Clean and inspect the Skimmer, Pump, and Filter

These components work together to keep backyard ponds running smoothly and with minimal assistance. When cleaning a pond, we gain intimate access to them, giving us the ability to diagnose and fix any issues.

7. Vacuum out muck and debris

Vacuuming out the muck accumulated at the bottom of a pond is a crucial step in the cleaning process. This removes nutrients that would otherwise be used as food by algae.

8. Refill with a mix of pond water and tap water

To complete the cleaning, we fill the pond back up with a mix of its old water and dechlorinated tap water. We then acclimate the fish with their new water and return them to their homes.



Frequently Asked Questions

If you've had your pond for a while and are having problems with water clarity, the most likely culprit is your filtration. Filtration in your pond can take many forms, including filter media (mats, lava rock, bioballs, sponges) or more natural elements such as plants, bacteria, and so on. If you are experiencing a sudden burst of cloudy water, it is possible that you recently added more fish than your pond can handle, or your fish have outgrown the filtration that was once adequate for your pond. A water change is the simplest solution to this problem; however, if the water becomes cloudy again in a short period of time, you must increase the filtration inside your pond.

The majority of algae in ponds is completely harmless and becomes a problem only when it becomes too excessive. Just like plants, algae need nutrients and sunlight to grow so reducing these elements will also reduce algae. You can do this by introducing surface blocking plants such as hyacinths and waterlilies. These plants not only provide shade, but also filter water (reducing nutrients) and create hiding spots for your fish. Plants are essential in keeping algae levels low.

Your fish will be safely relocated to a holding tank during the cleaning.
